The blackness was total. He did not know whether he was facing the telescreen or away from it. If he was facing it the guards could see him.

He lay as still as he could. He thought of O'Brien. He was not certain when he had first become aware of the possibility of betrayal. The Party had told him that it had reformed him, and he believed it.

Outside, even through the shut window pane, the world looked cold. Down in the street little eddies of wind were whirling dust and torn paper into spirals, and though the sun was shining and the sky was a harsh blue there seemed to be no colour in anything.

The telescreen was giving forth an ear-splitting whistle which continued on the same note for thirty seconds. It was nought seven fifteen, getting-up time for office workers.
